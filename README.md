# Understanding Suicide Through Global Data, 1985–2016

## Overview

This project explores global suicide trends from 1985–2016, focusing on how rates vary across time, country, gender, age group, GDP, and Human Development Index.

The project was completed as part of DATA 612 at American University. It emphasizes exploratory data analysis, visualization, statistical interpretation, and public-health communication.

## Research Questions

- How have suicide rates changed globally and in the United States over time?
- Do suicide trends differ by gender and age group?
- How does the United States compare to countries with historically high suicide rates?
- Are broad development indicators such as GDP and Human Development Index associated with suicide rates?

## Key Takeaways

- U.S. suicide rates diverged from global trends after approximately 2000.
- Increases were especially visible among men and working-age adults.
- GDP and HDI were statistically significant but weak predictors of suicide rates.
- Findings reinforce that suicide is multi-determined and requires multi-level interpretation.

## Notes

This project is exploratory and should not be interpreted as a causal analysis. The goal was to use reproducible analysis and visualization to communicate broad public-health patterns.
